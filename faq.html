FAQ

1. What is WiCV workshop?

The half-day workshop on Women in Computer Vision (WiCV) is a gathering for both women and men working in computer vision. The goals are to raise visibility of female computer vision researchers, to give opportunities to junior female students to present their work, and to provide a networking environment.

2. I don't identify as a woman. Can I still attend WiCV?

Yes, all genders are welcome to attend! Note however, all speakers and poster presenters will be people who identify as women, as our goal is to promote women in computer vision.

3. How can I participate in the workshop? Does attending WiCV require registering for CVPR?

Yes. WiCV is a CVPR workshop, so your registration for it is handeld via the CVPR website . Make sure your CVPR registration includes workshops.

4. Can anyone attend the poster session and the talks at the WiCV workshop?

Yes. The poster session and the talks are open to all CVPR attendees.

5. What are the talks given by the keynote speakers about?

The keynote speakers will give technical talks about their research in computer vision.

6. Who will present at oral and poster sessions? Are there presenter instructions available?

Authors of all accepted abstracts (with or without travel grant) will present their work in a poster session. A few abstracts will also be selected for oral presentation. We will add presenter instructions soon.

7. What is the WiCV dinner?

The dinner event is an opportunity to meet other female computer vision researchers. Poster presenters will be matched with senior computer vision researchers to share experience and career advice. The invitees will receive an e-mail and be asked to confirm attendance.

8. Can I attend WiCV dinner?

Unfortunately, the dinner is by invitation only.

9. How do I submit an abstract?

Create a submission through WiCV 2017 CMT page and follow the instructions. You will be asked to upload a 2-page abstract of your work in computer vision in final CVPR paper template . Submissions should include the abstract (in pdf), and optionally the resume (in pdf) of the author. The decisions will be made based only on the abstract. Authors should answer two questions: whether they give permission to share their resume with industry sponsors and whether they are applying for travel grant or not.

10. Do I have to submit a resume?

No, resume submission is optional. The decisions will be made based only on the abstract. If you choose to share your resume with industry sponsors, you should upload it on CMT.

11. Can I submit my previously published work? Can I submit my CVPR’17 work? Can I submit my unpublished work?

Yes. You can submit an abstract describing new, previously or concurrently published research.

12. Does a WiCV accepted abstract count as publication?

No, we do not have proceedings and the review process is not double-blind.

13. Is it still possible to submit an abstract after the deadline?

We are sorry, but late submissions are not accepted at the WiCV workshop. We would appreciate to stay in touch with us for future events and follow the updates of our workshop via our facebook group

14. How do I apply for travel grant?

You need to answer ‘Yes’ to the question ‘Are you applying for a travel stipend?’ on CMT when submitting your abstract. Note that you will be asked to provide a receipt for your travel expenses.

15. I don’t have an abstract, can I receive a travel grant?

No, we are able to give travel grants based on submitted abstracts.

16. I already received a travel grant from previous years’ WiCV workshop, can I apply again?

Yes, but we give less priority on last years’ attendees who received a travel grant.

17.If my abstract submission is accepted, am I guaranteed to receive a travel grant?

No, some abstracts are accepted without travel grants.

18. If my abstract submission is accepted with a travel grant but I don’t attend the workshop, can I receive the grant?

No, you must attend the workshop in order to receive a travel grant.

19. How much is the travel grant?

Final grant amounts will depend on the amount of sponsorship, the number of applications received and the location of the grant receiver. Last year travel grants ranged from $200-$800.

20. What will be covered by the travel grant? Do we need to submit receipts?

You can spend the travel grant for expenses needed to attend the workshop (e.g. plane ticket, accommodation, CVPR registration). Note that you will be asked to provide a receipt for your travel expenses. The grant might not cover all your expenses though.

21. I am awarded a travel grant. When and how will I receive travel grant?

If you attended the workshop and provided receipts, you will receive a grant. Collecting receipts and processing the payments might take time. The grants will be sent after the workshop. You may choose to receive the grant as check or wire transfer. We will get in touch with you for the process.

22. Is there a possibility to get in touch with women from the computer vision community?

Yes, join our facebook group and get updates regarding the workshop and women in computer vision community.

23. I have a question that is not answered here. How can I reach you?

You can contact us via Facebook, Twitter or e-mail.
